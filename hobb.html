Hobb!

An AI-driven Character-based Activity App

This is an AI-driven group project aiming at recommending activities for university students who seek a richer social life, mainly based on their personalities analyzed by using artificial intelligence techniques. The finalized trained AI model and our aim were reflected by a mobile App. I mainly took roles in product brainstorming and user interface design.

  • Changpei Ou(me): Project manager | Mind maps | Brainstorming director | Prototyping (physical and 3D models)
  • Milo Simons: Game-play design | Scenario | COCD analysis
  • Jasmijn de Joode: Mind maps | Affinity diagram | Testing
  • Bram Hoesdemakers: Observation | Interview
  • Xuhui Wang: Prototyping

* Other activities, such as brainstorming, literature collecting, and qualitative data analysis were executed collectively.

⑥ Emotional Design:
“Re-memory” features a design that evokes users' emotions, “leading to a positive user experience” (Don Norman: “Emotional Design”; IxDF)

⑦ Adaptive Design:
Three playing modes for people with demantia in different stages

1. Battle mode (PwD in early/middle stage)

The hinge structure of the board enables a physical connection with another board, This allows two players to play against each other by flipping card sets on their respective board, which is a bit more challenging but adds to the gamification. Besides, by enlarging the number of cards, this mode underlines our notion of “sharing the memories”.

2. Normal mode (PwD in middle stage)

Using one board, this mode allows the PwD to play the game with caregivers/family members, who will lead the PwD to press the button to look at the hidden cards and touch the fabric on the wheel that corresponds to the content of the exposed card. Once a match is found, the retro TV display which is connected to the board through Bluetooth will play the corresponding video clip.

3. Leisure mode (PwD in late stage)

This mode simulates a normal activity: “card inserting”, which is common in various daily situations such as retrieving money in the ATM, entering/exiting a parking lot, and so on. Easily understandable, the “insert and play the video” gaming circle serves as a process of “simply enjoying wonderful moments in life” and/or “weaving pieces of memories together”.
